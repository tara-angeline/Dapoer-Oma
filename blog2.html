Food and Friends

Posted March 2, 2024

Out of the many languages in the world, I truly think that there is only one language that breaks all barriers. One that is so universal that everyone connects to it in a certain degree. That language is food :).

Today, I want to share with you a heartfelt reflection on the power of food and friendship, especially as an international student navigating life abroad. My journey from Indonesia to Ireland has been filled with new experiences, challenges, and most importantly, meaningful connections forged over shared meals and cultural exchanges.

Food has always been a universal language that transcends cultural barriers, bringing people together and fostering connections. As an Indonesian student studying in Ireland, I quickly realized that while the flavors and ingredients may differ, the joy and camaraderie that come from sharing a meal are universally understood.

One of the ways I've sought to bridge the gap between my Indonesian heritage and my new life in Ireland is through sharing traditional Indonesian dishes with my friends and peers. Whether it's a comforting bowl of soto ayam, aromatic ayam kuning, or spicy rendang, each dish tells a story, offering a glimpse into the rich culinary heritage and vibrant culture of Indonesia.

Beyond just sharing food, cooking together has become a wonderful avenue for cultural exchange. I've had the pleasure of introducing my friends to the art of making Indonesian dishes, teaching them about our unique spices, cooking techniques, and traditions. In return, I've been delighted to learn about other cuisine and traditions, exploring new flavors and techniques that have enriched my culinary skills.

For example, last semester we celebrated Christmas and the end of our exam season by having a dinner together. This dinner was a potluck where everyone brought a dish from their culture. Through these shared culinary experiences, I've forged meaningful friendships that transcend cultural differences. Cooking together, sharing stories, and laughing over meals have created lasting memories and bonds that I will cherish long after my time in Ireland.
