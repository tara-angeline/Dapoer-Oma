Pandan Cendol

  • Time: 30 minutes

  • Servings: 5 serves

  • Category: Dessert

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 suji leaf, for colour (substitute with green food dye)

  • 150 grams rice flour

  • 15 grams tapioca starch

  • 700ml water

  • 2 grams salt

  • Coconut milk, for serving

  • Ice cubes, for cooling

Kinca (palm sugar syrup):

  • 470 grams palm sugar, grated

  • 130 grams white sugar

  • 3 pandan leaves

  • 10 grams salt

  • Jackfruit pieces (optional)

INSTRUCTION

  1. Blend suji leaf and some water in a food processor. Strain and set aside.

  2. Put suji leaf extract, rice flour, tapioca starch, water, and salt into a pan and mix well.

  3. Turn on heat to medium high and cook until batter thickens and becomes sticky. This takes about 20-25 minutes.

  4. Prepare a big bowl with cold water and ice cubes for cooling.

  5. Prepare a collander. Pour cendol batter in collander and let it pour into ice water to harden. It should look like little green worms.

  6. Strain cendol and set aside.

  7. To make "kinca", put kinca ingredients in a pan and heat on medium heat. Mix well and set aside.

  8. Serve cendol with kinca and coconut milk.
