Dapoer Oma Tiek

Posted Feb 2, 2024

Many people have come to me asking what "Dapoer Oma" means. When they do, I always answer with a smile because "Dapoer Oma" is my love letter for my grandmother. Her name is Oma Tiek.

"Dapoer Oma" directly translate to "Grandma's Kicthen" in Indonesian and it encapsulates the warmth, love, and culinary heritage that I associate with my grandmother's cooking.

Growing up, my grandmother's kitchen was a place of magic, filled with the comforting aromas of spices, the sizzle of frying pans, and the laughter and stories shared over meals. For every birthday in the house, my grandmother would cook up a huge meal for everyone (including our neighbours!). It was where family and freinds gathered, traditions passed down, and where I first fell in love with the art of cooking.

Through "Dapoer Oma," I aim to honor my grandmother's legacy by sharing the authentic recipes and cherished memories that have shaped my culinary journey. Each recipe is a tribute to the flavors and traditions that have been lovingly preserved and passed down through generations.

These are some of my personal favorite of her recipes. From left to right is her famous galantine (a chicken meatloaf with a killer gravy!), her cozy Soto Kuning (yellow chicken soup with vermicelli noodles!), and her addicting Putu Ayu (pandan steamed cake with coconuts!). I absolutely adore these dishes and wish I could recreate them in Ireland.

I'm super excited to share these recipes with all of you! As I'm experimenting with more of her recipes, I will try to upload more recipes to this website!
